UX Rec

A — Collapse & Stack is the right call. It communicates progress through a shrinking grid (16→12→8→4 tiles), keeps every solved category visible for reference, and never occludes anything the player needs to see. The fade-out → reflow → slide-in sequence maps to the mental model of "I solved that group, now it's filed away." It also degrades perfectly on mobile and animates purely with transform + opacity, so Framer Motion's layout prop will make it silky. B and D are more cinematic, but both sacrifice spatial clarity for spectacle — fine for a one-time wow, frustrating on a daily puzzle you play for speed.
